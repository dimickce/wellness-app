Daily hamstring routine
5–10 min · pair with walks or evenings
Supine hamstring stretch
Lying on back, loop a strap or towel around one foot, extend leg toward ceiling. Safe and controlled for your hamstring injury.
Seated forward fold with support
Legs extended, bolster or folded blanket on thighs, rest torso forward. No pulling or straining — fully passive.
Reclined figure-four
On back, cross one ankle over opposite knee and let hip relax open. Targets piriformis and outer hip, which compensates for hamstring tightness.
Low lunge with bolster
Addresses hip flexors, which pull on the full hamstring chain when tight. Use a bolster under back knee for support.
Legs up the wall (Viparita Karani) ⭐ Start here
Sit sideways against a wall, swing legs up. Fully passive and restorative. Great final stretch and evening wind-down. If you only do one stretch, do this one.

Foam rolling routine
3–5 min · on movement days

Find a tender point and hold 60–90 sec — don't roll back and forth. Pair this with beet intake to support circulation and blood flow to muscles.

IT band · side of thigh
Roll slowly along the outer thigh. When you find a tender spot, pause and hold. Don't roll continuously.
Quads · front of thigh
Supports hamstring recovery and reduces compensatory tightness. Go slow and breathe through tender points.
Glutes + upper back
Add after strength days especially. "Your body is going to thank you tomorrow!" — Sammy

Why you felt rough at the start of Fit House
Totally normal — here's the science

In the first few minutes, your body uses slow-twitch fibers — small, endurance-based, not very powerful. As you keep going, fast-twitch fibers kick in and share the load.

Think of it like starting a job with 2 employees, then 10 more show up. Give it 5–10 minutes. It gets better every session, and the more consistently you train, the faster that transition happens.
